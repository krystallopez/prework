Recipe for Bistec Encbollado

(Also known as Steak and Onions)

Bistec Encebollado is a classic Puerto Rican dish. It is considered a very typical lunch or dinner recipe. In most Latin American countries, Spanish onions tend to have a sweeter flavor than many other onions that help to balance out the vinegar, garlic, and oregano, that you'll be using in this recipe. On the stove top, I like to cook my steak well done, while some would say that is a travesty, however, for this dish cooking the steak well done helps to keep the moisture locked in while in the slow cooker, ensuring a very tender piece of meat.

Here are the ingredients you are going to need:

    2 pounds of flank steak
    2 Spanish onions, sliced
    salt
    pepper
    2 garlic cloves
    1/4 cup of red wine vinegar
    1 cup of beef broth
    2 teaspoons of oregano
    1 tablespoon of cumin

Now, let's get cooking! :

  • Slice the steak into long thin strips and place them into the slow cooker. Season the steak with salt and pepper to taste.
  • Then, cover the steak with sliced onions and minced garlic.
  • In a small bowl mix together the vinegar, beef broth, oregano, and cumin. Then pour over the steak and onions.
  • Set your slow cooker to low and cook for 8 hours.
  • Once your steak is tender, turn off the slow cooker and serve with white rice and sliced avocado.
    • A little variation tip:
    1. To make this on a stove top, mix together red wine vinegar, 1/2 cup of beef broth, oregano, cumin, and garlic. Place the steak in the marinade, making sure it is completely coated. Cover and place in the refrigerator for about 8 hours.
    2. After marinating, heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a skillet and sauté onions for about 3 to 4 minutes. Pour the other 1/2 cup of beef broth into the pan and let the onions cook, absorbing the broth for another 7 to 10 minutes.
    3. Then remove the onions from the pan, and in the same pan cook the steak to your preferred temperature. Then serve and enjoy!
